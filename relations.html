Pakistan China Relations (Iron Brotherhood)

Nature: Historic and unwavering strategic partnership. Called “Iron Brothers”, Pakistan and China maintain one of the world’s closest international friendships. Key Aspects: 1-Strategic & Military Ties: Joint production of JF-17 Thunder Fighter Jets Pakistan uses Chinese tanks, drones, radar systems, and missile technology Regular joint military drills (Sea Guardians, Warriors) Deep coordination in defense research & training 2- Economic Partnership: CPEC (China-Pakistan Economic Corridor): Flagship project of China’s Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) Worth $62+ billion Includes energy plants, highways, railway tracks, and special economic zones Boosts infrastructure, jobs, and power generation in Pakistan 3- Gwadar Port (Sea-Pack Importance): Gwadar Port, developed by China, is a deep-sea port in Balochistan Provides China direct access to the Arabian Sea Strategically important for China’s trade bypassing Malacca Strait Aims to transform Gwadar into a global trade hub and naval foothold 4-Political & Diplomatic Coordination: Mutual support in Kashmir issue (China supports UN resolutions) Both oppose Indian expansionism Consistent support for Pakistan at UN Security Council and FATF Quote by Chinese leadership: “Pakistan is our Iron Brother, our relationship is higher than mountains and deeper than oceans.”

Pakistan Saudi Arabia Relations (Brotherly & Strategic Partnership)

Nature: Long-standing Islamic, economic, and defense-based friendship. Considered one of Pakistan’s closest allies. 1- Historical & Religious Ties: Saudi Arabia supported Pakistan during 1965 & 1971 wars diplomatically and financially. Spiritual bond through Islam’s holy sites — millions of Pakistani Muslims perform Hajj & Umrah annually. Pakistan was one of the first countries to recognize Saudi Arabia in 1947. 2- Economic Support: 3+ million Pakistanis work in Saudi Arabia (largest diaspora), sending billions in remittances. Saudi Arabia has given billions in financial aid and oil credit to Pakistan during economic crises (most recently in 2023–24). $20 billion in investment pledges, including Gwadar oil refinery and industrial zones. 3- Defense & Military Cooperation: Pakistan has trained Saudi armed forces since the 1980s. Pakistani troops were stationed in KSA during the Gulf War and continue in advisory roles. Both conduct joint military exercises and share intelligence cooperation. Pakistan maintains a “strategic reserve force” to defend Saudi sovereignty if needed. 4- Political Alignment: Saudi Arabia supports Pakistan’s position on Kashmir in the OIC and UN. Pakistan balances relations with both Iran and Saudi Arabia diplomatically.

Pakistan Turkey Relations (Two Nations, One Heart)

Nature: Deep-rooted brotherhood based on shared religion, cultural heritage, and strategic cooperation. 1- Historical Brotherhood: Pakistan supported Turkish War of Independence in the 1920s through Khilafat Movement funds. Turkey strongly supported Pakistan in wars with India and in Kashmir dispute. 2- Military & Defense Collaboration: Joint defense production: Turkish-built MILGEM corvettes for Pakistan Navy. Turkey provides tech and training to Pakistan’s defense industry (e.g., drones, electronics). Military exercises between Turkish and Pakistani forces. 3- Economic & Trade Relations: Trade volume around $1 billion+, with plans to increase it significantly. Agreement on preferential trade, tourism cooperation, and business exchange. 4- Media & Cultural Influence: Popularity of Turkish dramas like Ertugrul promoted cultural ties. Joint ventures in education and scholarships (e.g., Turkish Maarif Foundation schools in Pakistan). 5- Diplomatic Support: Turkey openly backs Pakistan on Kashmir, condemning India’s moves in Article 370. Both countries push for unity in the Muslim world and address Islamophobia globally.

Pakistan United States Relations (Allies with Strains)

Nature: Strategic, but often tense relationship influenced by geopolitics, war on terror, and India-US growing ties. 1- Historical Engagement: Allies during Cold War, SEATO/CENTO blocs Pakistan helped broker US-China relations in 1971 (Kissinger’s secret visit via Pakistan) Major non-NATO ally of the US 2- War on Terror: Post-9/11, Pakistan became frontline state in US-led war on terror. Received $33+ billion in military & civilian aid (2002–2018). Over 70,000 Pakistanis died due to terrorism & counterterrorism fallout. 3- Military & Security: Shared intelligence & counterterrorism operations (e.g., Taliban, Al-Qaeda) US drone strikes in tribal areas remain controversial Tensions over Osama bin Laden raid (2011) in Abbottabad 4- Current Status: Shift from military focus to economic, climate, and tech cooperation US still engages Pakistan for Afghanistan stability, regional counterterrorism, and China balance Pakistan seeks balanced ties with both US and China

Pakistan India Relations (Rivals and Neighbors)

Nature: Longstanding hostility centered on Kashmir, water disputes, border tensions, and ideology. 1- Conflicts & Wars: Fought four wars: 1947, 1965, 1971, and Kargil (1999) Continuous skirmishes at Line of Control (LoC) in Kashmir India blames Pakistan for cross-border militancy; Pakistan denies and highlights Indian atrocities in Kashmir 2- Attempts at Peace: Peace talks: Simla Agreement, Agra Summit, Lahore Declaration, Track II diplomacy Backchannel diplomacy continues intermittently (especially via UAE) 3- 2019 Fallout: India revoked Article 370 (removing Kashmir's autonomy) Pakistan downgraded diplomatic ties, suspended trade Rising extremism in India (e.g., Hindutva ideology) worsens relations 4- Water Disputes: Indus Waters Treaty (1960): Brokered by World Bank India threatens to alter water flow; Pakistan warns of escalation

Pakistan Iran Relations (Bordering Brotherhood with Caution)

Nature: Cultural and religious ties with geopolitical complexities. 1-Shared Values: Both are founding members of ECO (Economic Cooperation Organization) Both oppose sectarian violence and extremism 2- Border Security Issues: Militants operate on both sides (e.g., Jaish al-Adl, Baloch insurgents) Recent tensions resolved through high-level military communication 3- Gas Pipeline Project: Iran-Pakistan Gas Pipeline (Peace Pipeline) stalled due to US sanctions Iran threatens penalties if Pakistan delays further 4- Religious Sensitivities: Sunni-Shia dynamic carefully managed Avoiding proxy conflicts (e.g., Iran–Saudi rivalry impact)

Global Role: United Nations & OIC

1-UN Peacekeeping: Pakistan is top troop contributor to UN peacekeeping missions Recognized for professionalism, courage, and humanitarian help 2-OIC & Muslim Causes:Vocal on Kashmir, Palestine, and Islamophobia Promotes Muslim unity and justice in international forums
