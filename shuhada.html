Martyrs of Pakistan Army

Major Raja Aziz Bhatti

Martyred: 10 September 1965, near BRB Canal, Lahore

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Major Raja Aziz Bhatti was a valiant officer of the Pakistan Army who embraced martyrdom during the 1965 Indo-Pak War. He led the defense of the BRB Canal near Lahore for five continuous days and nights, under relentless enemy fire. Refusing to be relieved from the front lines, he fought bravely and inspired his troops until he was hit by an enemy tank shell.

His famous quote: "Do not recall me; I will not abandon my post as long as the enemy is there."

Captain-Karnal-Sher-Khan

Martyrdom:July 5, 1999

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Captain Karnal Sher Khan was a fearless officer of the Pakistan Army who fought bravely during the 1999 Kargil War. Despite being outnumbered, he led his troops with extraordinary courage and recaptured strategic positions at great personal risk. He embraced martyrdom while defending his post against heavy enemy attack. His heroism earned him the Nishan-e-Haider, Pakistan’s highest military award for valor.He led his men through intense Indian attacks, recapturing lost posts at high altitude under severe conditions. Even after being surrounded, he fought bravely till embracing shahadat (martyrdom) on July 5, 1999.

Sawar-muhammad-hussain

Martyrdom: December 10, 1971

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Sawar Muhammad Hussain was a brave soldier of the Pakistan Army who displayed outstanding courage during the 1971 Indo-Pak War. Despite being a driver, he volunteered for frontline duties and carried ammunition under intense enemy fire. He destroyed several enemy tanks and embraced martyrdom while directing fire towards enemy positions. For his extraordinary bravery, he was posthumously awarded the Nishan-e-Haider, Pakistan’s highest military honor.

Major-shabbir-sharif

Martyrdom:December 6, 1971

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Awarded with Sitara-e-Jurat (for bravery in the 1965 war)

He led his troops to capture a key position across the Sabuna distributary under intense enemy fire. Despite being wounded, he refused evacuation and continued to fight. He was martyred while destroying an enemy bunker. Legacy: Elder brother of General Raheel Sharif (former Chief of Army Staff, Pakistan Army). Only Pakistani to have received both Nishan-e-Haider and Sitara-e-Jurat.

Havaldar-lalak-jan

Martyrdom:July 7, 1999

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Havaldar Lalak Jan was a valiant soldier of the Northern Light Infantry who fought heroically during the Kargil conflict in 1999. Volunteering to serve at the front lines, he repulsed several Indian attacks on his post despite being seriously injured. Refusing evacuation, he continued fighting until he embraced martyrdom. His courage, determination, and devotion to duty earned him Pakistan's highest military honor, the Nishan-e-Haider.

Naik Saif Ali Janjua

Martyrdom : October 26, 1948

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Naik Saif Ali Janjua was a brave soldier of the Azad Kashmir Regiment who fought during the 1947–48 Indo-Pak War. He showed extraordinary courage while defending a strategic post at Pir Kalewa against repeated Indian attacks. Despite being heavily outnumbered and wounded, he continued to inspire his men and fought until his last breath. He embraced martyrdom on October 26, 1948.For his outstanding bravery and sacrifice, he was posthumously awarded the Hilal-e-Kashmir, which was later declared equivalent to the Nishan-e-Haider, making him an official recipient of Pakistan's highest military award.

Major Muhammad Akram Shaheed

Martyrdom: December 5, 1971

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Major Muhammad Akram served in the 4th Frontier Force Regiment and was deployed in the Hilli sector during the 1971 Indo-Pak war. He demonstrated outstanding leadership and valor while defending his position against a much larger Indian force. For several days, he and his troops held their ground under constant enemy attacks, showing exceptional courage and resilience.On December 5, 1971, he embraced martyrdom in the line of duty. His heroic actions, dedication, and supreme sacrifice earned him the Nishan-e-Haider, Pakistan’s highest military honor.

Major Tufail Muhammad Shaheed

Martyrdom: August 7, 1958

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Major Tufail Muhammad was posted in East Pakistan when Indian troops illegally crossed the border and occupied a Pakistani village in the Lakshmipur sector. On August 7, 1958, he led a daring mission to expel the intruding forces. Despite being seriously wounded by enemy fire, he continued to fight bravely, killing several enemy soldiers in close combat.Even in his final moments, he kept giving orders to his men and ensured the mission's success. He embraced martyrdom after completing his objective. For his unparalleled bravery and leadership, he was awarded the Nishan-e-Haider, the highest gallantry award of Pakistan.

Pilot Officer Rashid Minhas Shaheed

Martyrdom: August 20, 1971

Awrded with Nishan-e-Haider

Pilot Officer Rashid Minhas was undergoing training at PAF Base Masroor when he displayed extraordinary bravery at the age of just 20. On August 20, 1971, during a routine training flight in a T-33 jet, his instructor, a defecting pilot, tried to hijack the aircraft and take it to India.Realizing the threat, Rashid Minhas fought to regain control mid-air. Knowing the consequences, he deliberately crashed the aircraft 32 miles from the Indian border to prevent the betrayal. He embraced shahadat and became the youngest and only Pakistan Air Force officer to receive the Nishan-e-Haider.His sacrifice symbolizes unmatched patriotism and courage.

Lance Naik Muhammad Mahfuz

Martyrdom: December 18, 1971

Awarded with Nishan-e-Haider

Lance Naik Muhammad Mahfuz of the 15 Punjab Regiment displayed unmatched bravery during the Indo-Pak War of 1971. Despite being seriously wounded by enemy fire, he advanced alone towards an enemy bunker with sheer determination.He crawled under heavy fire and, even after receiving a fatal injury, engaged the enemy hand-to-hand, disabling their bunker before embracing martyrdom. His incredible courage and sacrifice earned him Pakistan’s highest military gallantry award — the Nishan-e-Haider.

Captain Bilal Zafar

Martyrdom: May 17, 2009

Awarded with Sitara-e-Basalat

Captain Bilal Zafar of the elite SSG (Special Services Group) was a fearless commando who laid down his life during an operation against terrorists in Swat. He led his team into a militant stronghold and engaged in intense close-quarter combat. Despite sustaining injuries, he continued to fight bravely until he was martyred while neutralizing key enemy positions.His leadership, courage, and selfless sacrifice earned him the Sitara-e-Basalat posthumously. He is remembered as the "Hero of Swat".

Captain Aikash Rabbani

Martyrdom: September 25, 2023

Awarded with Tamgha-e-Basalat

Captain Aikash Rabbani of the Pakistan Army embraced martyrdom in the line of duty while bravely combating terrorists during an intelligence-based operation in North Waziristan. Known for his fearless leadership and deep patriotism, he led his team into hostile territory and fought valiantly until he was fatally wounded.His unwavering courage, commitment to peace, and sacrifice symbolize the devotion of Pakistan Army officers who defend the nation with honor. Captain Rabbani is remembered as a true hero who laid down his life for the protection of his homeland.

Lieutenant Arsalan Alam

Martyrdom: September 23, 2017

Awarded with Sitara-e-Basalat

Lieutenant Arsalan Alam was the commanding officer of the newly established Pakistani border post in Rajgal Valley when it came under heavy terrorist fire from across the Afghan border. Despite the danger, he bravely held his position and coordinated a defense to repel the attack. He was hit by a sniper bullet and embraced martyrdom at the age of 22.His courage and leadership, even in the face of certain danger, earned him the Sitara-e-Basalat, honoring his unwavering dedication to the country.

Captain Asfandyar Bukhari

Martyrdom: September 18, 2015

Award: Sitara-e-Basalat

Captain Asfandyar Bukhari was martyred during a terrorist attack on the Pakistan Air Force Base in Badaber, Peshawar. He was part of the Quick Reaction Force that bravely responded to the attack. While leading his men in clearing the base and rescuing personnel under fire, he embraced martyrdom in the line of duty.He displayed exceptional bravery, leadership, and devotion to his mission, which earned him the Sitara-e-Basalat for gallantry and service to the nation.

Captain Umair Abbasi

Martyrdom: November 3, 2015

Awarded with Tamgha-e-Basalat

Captain Umair Abbasi was a courageous young officer of the Pakistan Army who embraced martyrdom while fighting militants in Khyber Agency during a counter-terrorism operation. Despite heavy enemy fire, he led from the front to secure a critical area. His selfless sacrifice and determination in the face of danger inspired his comrades and upheld the highest traditions of military valor.He was honored with the Tamgha-e-Basalat in recognition of his bravery and sacrifice for the motherland

Flying Officer Marium Mukhtiar

Martyrdom: November 24, 2015

Awarded with Tamgha-e-Basalat

Flying Officer Marium Mukhtiar was the first female fighter pilot of the Pakistan Air Force to embrace martyrdom in the line of duty. While flying a routine training mission in a FT-7PG aircraft, she and her co-pilot encountered a critical malfunction. Demonstrating remarkable presence of mind, she stayed with the aircraft until it was safely away from populated areas, sacrificing her life to save civilians.Her courage, professionalism, and ultimate sacrifice made her a symbol of pride and inspiration for the entire nation. She was posthumously awarded the Tamgha-e-Basalat for her gallantry.
